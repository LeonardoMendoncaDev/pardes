I am **Leonardo Mendonça**, software architect. I live between Portugal and Brazil and have worked for several years on systems of considerable size — systems with accumulated technical debt, real users, fragile integrations between legacy modules and new construction. It is from prolonged contact with this kind of problem that PARDES is born.

The methodology was distilled over two years of application in parallel projects of great complexity. I started designing it out of personal need — I needed a way of operating that would survive rapid context switches, calls that arrive without warning, teams with different levels of technical maturity, and the growing use of artificial intelligence agents in the development cycle. What I share here is what, at the end of those two years, remained as firm residue — what continues to work.

I do not present myself as a guru nor as a theorist. I am a practitioner. The methodology lives because it is applied every day. The material on this site will grow as the work justifies it, and only when it justifies it — I prefer to write little and truthfully than to write much to occupy space.
